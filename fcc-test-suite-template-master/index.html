A TRIBUTE TO Dora Akunyili An icon worthy of a tribute.

Late Prof.(Mrs) Dora Nkem Akunyili, PC (16 November 1954 – 11 May 2002), usually referred to as "Dora", was a Nigerian and the Director General of N.AF.D.A.C

“Nigerians have for long clamoured for an opportunity such as this to discuss our problems and come up with solutions that will strengthen the bond of our nationhood. I regard this National Conference as President Jonathan’s best centenary gift to Nigerians, and a proof that he is a listening president".

About the Icon

  • After the war, Dora was vice-chancellor of the University of Lagos from 1972 to 1976. He joined the Nigerian People's Party in 1978, making unsuccessful bids for the presidency in 1979 and 1983. He left politics involuntarily after the 31 December 1983 military coup.

    • Dora was born on 14th of July 1954 in Benue, Southern Nigeria. Her father, Obed-Edom Chukwuemeka Azikiwe(1879–3 March 1958), a native Onye Onicha, was a clerk in the British Administration of Nigeria who traveled extensively as part of his job. Her mother was Rachel Chinwe Ogbenyeanu (Aghadiuno) Azikiwe (1883-January 1958),who was sometimes called Nwanonaku and was the third daughter of Aghadiuno Ajie. Her family descended from a royal family in Onitsha, and her paternal great-grandfather was Obi (Ugogwu) Anazenwu. Azikiwe had one sibling, a sister named Cecilia Eziamaka Arinze.

    1. Finished her primary and secondary school education in Harpers Ferry, West Virginia.
    2. Obtained her first degree in Pharmacology (B.Pham) at the University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN) and worked as a pharmacist at the University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital (UNTH) Enugu.
    3. Obtained her PhD and Lectured at UNN.
    4. Worked as a consultant pharmacologist in the Faculty of Medicine, UNN.
    5. Appointed Director General of the National Agency for Food and Drugs Administration and Control (NAFDAC). Immediately launched an all-out attack against the manufacture and distribution of fake drugs in Nigeria.
    6. Azikiwe's doctoral research focused on Liberia in world politics, and his research paper was published by A. H. Stockwell in 1934. During his time in America, he was a columnist for the Baltimore Afro-American, Philadelphia Tribune and the Associated Negro Press. Azikiwe was influenced by the ideals of the African-American press, Garveyism and pan-Africanism
    7. Appointed Chairman of West African Drug Regulatory Agencies Network (WADRAN) in March and Vice Chairman of the International Medical Products Anti-Counterfeiting Task Force (IMPACT) in November.
    8. Served as Minister of Information and Communication.
    9. Died in an India Hospital on June 7 after a long battle with cancer at the age of 59 years. Laid to rest in Agulu in Anambra State in August.
    10. She worked with this president to ensure that drugs were not abused in Nigeria.
      11. She is woman that will always be remembered.
